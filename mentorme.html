THE PROBLEM

Students often find themselves feeling alone and stressed out while trying to pursue academic or professional endeavors. The feeling of being on your own is overwhelming. These students need a way to focus on building meaningful, personal relationships to succeed professionally. Our user base is college students as well as recent graduates and working professionals. The core interaction is the ability to actively search for and message a mentor/mentee in the same field.

INITIAL IMPLEMENTATION

While the initial sketch of the application was made in a web format, it soon became apparent that MentorMe would be most useful as a mobile application. We interviewed 20 students at UCSD and 19/20 recommended to make MentorMe primarily a mobile application. The initial web implementation was not scalable (see bottom right) and this was adapted in future iterations (see "Mobile Iterations").

A/B USER TESTING

A/B User Testing was performed on two home pages: a scrolling view and a swiping view. The amount of times a user clicked on a profile was tracked using Google Analytics. The statistical results from the A/B testing gave a p value of roughly 0.95, meaning that there is not a significant difference between the number of profiles which are viewed on either home page. We discovered in our user testing interviews that scrolling is preferred, so this was implemented.

IMPACT

MentorMe has the potential to change the lives of many students and potential mentors around the world. Young students are often lost, and a well-matched mentor will change their professional life. As a byproduct, the mentor will also benefit. A younger individual in a mentor's network will bring new and innovate ideas of the next generation. MentorMe will change professional lives forever.
