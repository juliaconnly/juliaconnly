Cookbook



1 Month Student Project
My Role: UX Designer
Problem: need for a professional
            cookbook
Solution: personalized book
Marketed toward individuals who show interest in Chinese-American cuisine


THE PROBLEM

Families around the world use cookbooks to share their favorite recipes and pass them down generations. These families need a way to share their favorite recipes with distant relatives and friends. This cookbook was designed for individuals who share an interest in Chinese-American cuisine and allows people to share well-written and visually annotated recipes.

IMPLEMENTATION

The cookbook was designed using Shutterfly's "advanced editing" design interface. This required importing personalized backgrounds, creating a cusotmized text layouts, and styling imported pictures with corresponding text.



TOUGHEST DECISIONS


The most difficult decision involved designing a layout for each page which represented the particular recipes. The Honey-Glazed Walnuts, for example, required many steps in which illustrations were helpful. The column of images with sequenced steps was intentionally designed to improve usability of the cookbook.


Another difficult decision involves Nielsen's heuristic of consistency. It was important to keep in mind that each recipe required a different amount of images. However, the location of important information such as an overview, cooking time, and servings was purposfeully placed at the top of each recipe page. The font, size, and color of the heading remains consistent across each page. In addition, the overview, cooking time, and servings are consistent in color and location.


IMPACT

This cookbook has an impact on the individuals who wish to create and share Chinese-American cuisine with family and friends. It was carefully constructed to provide clear directions with an intuitive table of contents and layout design for a variety of potential chefs.
